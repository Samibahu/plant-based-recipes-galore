Palak Pakora

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch spinach leaves
  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Wash and pat dry the spinach leaves. Set aside.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, rice flour, carom seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  3. Add water gradually to form a smooth, thick batter.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Dip each spinach leaf into the batter, ensuring it is well coated.
  6. Carefully drop the coated leaves into the hot oil and fry until golden and crisp.
  7. Remove the pakoras from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve hot with chutney or ketchup.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 pakora

Calories: 80 kcal

Protein: 2 g

Carbohydrates: 9 g

Cooking Tips and Tricks

1. Ensure the oil is hot enough before frying to achieve crispy pakoras. You can test this by dropping a small bit of batter into the oil; it should sizzle and rise to the surface.

2. For extra crispiness, add a pinch of baking soda to the batter.

4. Use fresh spinach leaves for the best flavor and texture.

5. If the batter is too thick, add a little more water. It should coat the leaves well but not be runny.

Pudina Chutney

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Add a small amount of water and blend until smooth. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  4. Transfer the chutney to a serving bowl. Serve with snacks like samosas or pakoras.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 2 tablespoons

Calories: 25 kcal

Protein: 1 g

Carbohydrates: 5 g

Fat: 0 g

Sodium: 50 mg

Cooking Tips and Tricks

1. Use fresh mint and coriander for the best flavor. Avoid using dried herbs as they lack the vibrant taste of fresh herbs.

2. Adjust the number of green chilies based on your preferred spice level. For a milder chutney, use fewer chilies or remove the seeds.

3. Adding a small piece of sugar or a bit of honey can balance the flavors if the chutney is too tangy or spicy.

4. If the chutney is too thick, add water gradually until you reach your desired consistency. It should be smooth and easily spreadable.

5. For an extra kick, you can add a pinch of roasted cumin powder or chaat masala to the chutney before serving.

Vanilla Bean Crème Brûlée

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 vanilla bean, split and scraped
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar (for caramelizing)

Cooking Process

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Heat the heavy cream and vanilla bean in a saucepan until it just starts to simmer. Remove from heat and let steep for 15 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and granulated sugar until pale and slightly thickened. Gradually add the warm cream mixture, whisking constantly.
  3. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a measuring cup. Divide the custard between ramekins.
  4. Place the ramekins in a baking dish and add hot water to the dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the custards are set but still slightly jiggly in the center. Remove from the oven and let cool. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  6. Before serving, sprinkle the tops with light brown sugar and use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until it is golden and crispy.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 ramekin

Calories: 320 kcal

Protein: 6 g

Carbohydrates: 25 g

Fat: 22 g

Sodium: 70 mg

Cooking Tips and Tricks

1. Make sure the heavy cream does not boil; it should just simmer to avoid curdling.

2. Use a vanilla bean for the best flavor, but you can substitute with vanilla extract if needed (about 2 teaspoons).

3. Strain the custard mixture thoroughly to remove any coagulated egg bits for a smooth texture.

4. Be careful not to over-bake the crème brûlée. It should be slightly jiggly in the center when done.

5. For a perfect caramelized top, hold the kitchen torch a few inches above the sugar and move in a circular motion to ensure even browning.

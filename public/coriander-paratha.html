Coriander Parantha

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Water as needed
  • Ghee or oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the wheat flour, chopped coriander leaves, cumin seeds, salt, and turmeric powder.
  2. Add water gradually and knead into a soft dough.
  3. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each into a ball.
  4. Roll each ball into a flat circle, dusting with flour as needed.
  5. Heat a griddle or tawa over medium heat. Cook each parantha, applying ghee or oil on both sides until golden brown.
  6. Serve hot with curd or pickle.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 parantha

Calories: 180 kcal

Protein: 4 g

Carbohydrates: 30 g

Fat: 4 g

Sodium: 250 mg

Cooking Tips and Tricks

1. Ensure the dough is soft and pliable; if it's too stiff, the paranthas may turn out hard.

2. You can add a pinch of ajwain (carom seeds) to the dough for extra flavor.

3. For a crispier texture, roll the dough thinner.

4. Use a rolling pin and work surface dusted with flour to prevent sticking.

5. If the parantha starts to puff up while cooking, gently press it down with a spatula for even cooking.

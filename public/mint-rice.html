Mint Rice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak for 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and bay leaf, and saut√© for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onions and green chilies, and cook until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add the finely chopped mint leaves and cook for another minute.
  5. Add the soaked and drained rice to the pan, and stir gently to mix with the mint and onion mixture.
  6. Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  7. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving.

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 220 kcal per serving
  • Carbohydrates: 38g
  • Proteins: 4g
  • Fats: 6g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • Vitamins: A, C, and E (from mint and other ingredients)

Cooking Tips & Tricks

  • Rinsing Rice: Always rinse your rice thoroughly to remove excess starch. This ensures the rice doesn't become too sticky.
  • Soaking Rice: Soaking the rice helps to shorten the cooking time and make the grains fluffier.
  • Mint Leaves: Use fresh mint leaves for the best flavor. Dried mint can be used as a substitute, but fresh mint gives a more vibrant taste.
  • Aromatics: Adding whole spices like bay leaves and cumin seeds adds depth to the flavor of the dish.
  • Resting Time: Letting the rice rest covered after cooking helps to further steam it and fluff up the grains before serving.
